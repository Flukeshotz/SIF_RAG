What are the specific exit load regulations for Social Impact Funds (SIF) under SEBI guidelines?

SIF Regulations SEBI Framework 2023

Under the SEBI (Alternative Investment Funds) Regulations, 2012 (specifically amended for Social Impact Funds), exit loads are strictly regulated to ensure capital remains committed to social outcomes. A Social Impact Fund (SIF) is generally permitted to levy an exit load on investors, provided specific conditions are met and disclosed upfront.

Key Findings

  • Maximum Cap: SEBI regulations do not explicitly mandate a numeric cap on exit loads for SIFs, unlike mutual funds; however, they require the exit load structure to be fully detailed in the Private Placement Memorandum (PPM) [1].
  • Tenure Alignment: Given SIFs are close-ended funds (minimum tenure of 3 years), premature exits are heavily discouraged. Any exit load levied must be credited back to the fund, not the Investment Manager [2].
  • Social Impact Caveat: For grants specifically (a unique feature of SIFs), exit mechanics generally do not apply as grants are non-refundable commitments [3].
info

Regulatory Note: Recent circulars (2023) emphasize that any changes to the exit load structure post-onboarding require consent from at least 75% of investors by value.
