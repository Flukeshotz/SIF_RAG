The Wealth Company Flexi Cap Fund FAQs

Flexi Cap Mutual Funds are open-ended equity schemes that invest across large-cap, mid-cap, and small-cap stocks without any restriction on allocation to market cap limits. This gives fund managers the flexibility to dynamically manage the portfolio based on market conditions.

Ideal for investors seeking diversification and long-term capital growth. Suitable for those with a high-risk appetite and a 3–5-year investment horizon.

They offer diversified portfolios with a potential for lower risk than mid and small cap funds and potential for better returns than a large cap fund.

The stated objective for the fund is to To provide long-term growth in capital and income to investors, through active management of investments in a diversified portfolio of equity and equity-related securities across the entire market capitalization spectrum and in debt and money market instruments. There is no assurance or guarantee that the investment objective of the Scheme will be achieved. The benchmark index is the Nifty 500 TRI Index.

Direct Plan: Direct plan is what you buy directly from the Mutual Fund Regular Plan: A Regular plan is what you buy through an Mutual Fund Distributor Flexi Cap Fund provides the following 2 options to invest Growth Option Income distribution cum withdrawal (IDCW) Option – Payout and Reinvestment IDCW Payout: When Mutual Fund scheme realize gains or generate accruals in their portfolios, they distribute part of such gain/accrual to their investors by way of dividends. IDCW Reinvestment: This is similar to dividend payout except that the dividend declared is re-invested in the same scheme on the same day’s NAV. So, all your dividend is invested back in the same scheme and you receive additional units.

Default option of the scheme is Growth. In case investor opts for IDCW(Dividend), then IDCW payout will be the default option, if nothing specified by the investor
